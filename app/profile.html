Purpose

The impact that malicious Twitter bots, or automated Twitter accounts intent on appearing to be a real human Twitter Users, have had on worldwide democratic elections is now an indisputable fact. Malicious and benign actors infiltration of hot-button, timely issues has a well documented history across the globe now (CNN: Twitter Bots Double Ahead of Swedish 2018 Elections).

TAT is a third-party Twitter Analysis Tool to help study these bots. It takes a User-supplied Hashtag, retrieves recent posts containing that hashtag, and utilizes machine learning models to automatically detect potential Tweets from bots.

We then generate a report that contains a breakdown of likely real users’ vs. potential bots’ Tweets, and the rehydratable Twitter IDs of the potential bots’ posts. The report is a txt or pdf file, and the rehydratable Twitter IDs will be logged in our database, as well as in a separate csv file.
